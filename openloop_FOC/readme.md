A three-phase electric motor needs 3-phase Alternating Current (AC) for rotation. But, the sources, such as batteries or solar panel, only provide Direct Current (DC). So, an inverter is used to convert the DC to AC so that the electric motor can be operated. <br>

A three-phase voltage source inverter consists of three half-bridges, each containing two power transistors (eg. IGBTs or MOSFETs). The inverter can only adopt discrete switching states; intermediate voltage levels are realized by rapidly toggling these states, i.e., by pulse width modulation (PWM).
